# Mechanical performance and circular valorization of false selvedge from industrial weaving waste: a case study in Southern Brazil

## Abstract

<jats:sec>
                    <jats:title>Purpose</jats:title>
                    <jats:p>This paper aims to investigate the mechanical performance and circular valorization of false selvedge waste from industrial weaving.</jats:p>
                  </jats:sec>
                  <jats:sec>
                    <jats:title>Design/methodology/approach</jats:title>
                    <jats:p>False selvedge was collected, braided into different configurations, and subjected to tensile strength testing.</jats:p>
                  </jats:sec>
                  <jats:sec>
                    <jats:title>Findings</jats:title>
                    <jats:p>Three-strand braids demonstrated the highest tensile strength, offering promising reuse alternatives for textile waste.</jats:p>
                  </jats:sec>
                  <jats:sec>
                    <jats:title>Research limitations/implications</jats:title>
                    <jats:p>Results are based on samples from one facility; further testing is needed for broader applicability.</jats:p>
                  </jats:sec>
                  <jats:sec>
                    <jats:title>Practical implications</jats:title>
                    <jats:p>Reprocessed selvedge can be used in eco-design and semi-industrial products.</jats:p>
                  </jats:sec>
                  <jats:sec>
                    <jats:title>Social implications</jats:title>
                    <jats:p>The reuse model can empower small businesses and cooperatives through the production of value-added goods, promoting local employment and circular microeconomies.</jats:p>
                  </jats:sec>
                  <jats:sec>
                    <jats:title>Originality/value</jats:title>
                    <jats:p>This study presents an innovative reuse route for a commonly discarded textile residue, using low-energy, low-tech processes to create mechanically functional braided structures. It fills a gap in literature by proposing scalable, material-efficient valorization for industrial weaving waste.</jats:p>
                  </jats:sec>

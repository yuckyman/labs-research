## Description

The Max Planck Institute for Biological Cybernetics investigates information processing in the brain, with particular emphasis on how the brain perceives, learns, and remembers. The institute combines theoretical neuroscience, systems neuroscience, and computational approaches to understand cognitive processes.

Research at the institute spans multiple scales, from single neurons to complex behavioral systems, utilizing cutting-edge neuroimaging, psychophysics, and computational modeling techniques.

## Research Focus

- **Perception and Cognition** - Visual processing, multisensory integration
- **Computational Neuroscience** - Neural network models, learning algorithms
- **Systems Neuroscience** - Brain imaging, neural population dynamics
- **Behavioral Neuroscience** - Decision-making, motor control
- **Machine Learning** - Biological intelligence, artificial neural networks

## Facilities

- 3T and 9.4T MRI scanners
- Motion capture laboratories
- Virtual reality systems
- High-performance computing clusters
- Behavioral testing facilities
